About Us

Why We Call Ourselves "Angry Feminists"

The Angry Feminist is a platform for radical feminist consciousness-raising and analysis of contemporary culture and politics. We are a group of university students based in Montreal. We started The Angry Feminist to share our opinions with readers and offer a radical and queer-centered viewpoint that is missing from mainstream feminist discussions. We aim to challenge both patriarchal norms and mainstream feminist culture, leaving no reasoning unquestioned and no argument unexamined. We hope to inspire all readers to become angry feminists. We believe everyone should support equality between the sexes and refuse to accept anything else. Although being an ‘angry feminist’ is often used as a criticism, we see this as a point of pride.

“I am angry. Gender as it functions today is a grave injustice. We should all be angry. Anger has a long history of bringing about positive change; but, in addition to being angry, I’m also hopeful. Because I believe deeply in the ability of human beings to make and remake themselves for the better.” -Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie

An angry feminist is someone who refuses to passively accept the status-quo limitations placed on women in society, the workplace, and the home. An angry feminist is someone who is willing to have uncomfortable conversations and confront injustice. We look for inspiration from angry feminists throughout history and the present, including Sojourner Truth, Susan B Anthony, Audre Lorde, Ruth Bader Ginsburg, Malala Yousafzai, AOC, and more. Anger is a taboo for many feminists and civil rights activists, but we think anger is a rational response to injustice and a useful means for enacting change.

Anger therefore reflects our dedication to creating a more equitable world. Being an angry feminist reflects our commitment to creating a more just and equal society, something we believe everyone should commit to. We want to make gender equality and feminism inclusive for all people. Feminism is not just for women. Men are hurt by toxic masculinity and standards of stoicism and machismo.
